The Data

Four metrics that matter. Each tells a different part of the story.

Regional Employment Divergence

Why this matters: If AI fluency spreads broadly, economic benefits distribute and these lines converge. If fluency concentrates in metros, the lines diverge. The persistent 1.5 to 2.5 percentage point gap between Northern Virginia and Southside is the economic signature of uneven capability distribution.

Source: Bureau of Labor Statistics, Local Area Unemployment Statistics. County-level data aggregated to Weldon Cooper Center regions.

How People Use AI: Automation vs. Augmentation

Why this matters: Augmentation means people are thinking with AI, building, iterating, using it as a judgment partner. Automation means AI is doing tasks for people without deeper engagement. The ratio between these two modes is the clearest measure of whether adoption is becoming fluency or staying shallow.

Source: Anthropic Economic Index (Hugging Face, CC-BY). US national data from Claude conversations. Reports: Feb 2025, Sep 2025, Jan 2026.

Geographic Spread of AI Usage

Why this matters: The Gini coefficient measures how evenly AI usage is distributed across US states. Lower means more equal. A falling Gini means AI is spreading geographically, which favors the distributed scenarios. A rising Gini means concentration, which favors "Pockets of Excellence" or "The Widening Gap."

The Fluency Gap: What AI Could Do vs. What It Does

Why this matters: This is the fluency question visualized. The gap between what AI is theoretically capable of in each occupation and what businesses actually use it for represents unrealized potential. In "Build It Yourself," the green bars grow across all categories. In "Software Does It For You," they grow only where vendors embed AI. In "Pockets of Excellence," they grow only in metro knowledge-worker categories.

Source: Anthropic Economic Index + Eloundou et al. (2023) theoretical capability scores. Observed data from real Claude usage in professional contexts.

High reported AI "adoption" with low fluency depth
Census BTOS, Anthropic Economic Index
Only 4% of occupations use AI for 75%+ of tasks. 36% use it for 25%+. The gap between "using AI" and "fluent in AI" is wide and measurable.
This is the signature of shallow adoption: everyone reports using AI, almost nobody has built anything with it.
SaaS platforms shipping AI features that absorb business intelligence
Industry observation
QuickBooks, Toast, ServiceTitan, Dentrix, Procore, AppFolio all shipped embedded AI in 2025-2026. Each learns from business data, deepening lock-in.
When AI features absorb business intelligence into vendor platforms, switching costs become existential. The floor rises but becomes a ceiling.
Automation ratio rising faster than augmentation
Anthropic Economic Index
Aug 2025: automation briefly overtook augmentation for the first time. Nov 2025: swung back. API usage is 75% automation. Trend is mixed.
High automation + low augmentation = AI doing tasks for people, not people learning to think with AI.
Policy urgency low despite adoption metrics
BuildFirst field observations
GO Virginia has not prioritized AI fluency programming. Community colleges on standard 12-18 month timelines. No emergency posture.
When adoption metrics look strong, policymakers see no urgency. The institutional vacuum persists.
Task coverage broad but shallow (routine tasks only)
Anthropic Economic Index
36% of occupations use AI for 25%+ of tasks, but usage concentrates in routine categories. Deep judgment-augmentation remains rare.
Broad but shallow = the platform scenario. AI everywhere but not doing anything vendors couldn't have shipped.
